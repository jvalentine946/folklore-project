El Duende

El Duende is a creature created by Latin Americans who believe this elf/goblin creature actually exists and lures in Mexico’s forests and caves. El Duende is believed to be evil and good for its reasons. It is also heard that it can take the shape of older people or helpless people and requires help/assistance.

There is no exact origin of El Duende but they seem to be mistaken for spider monkeys or howler monkeys, even though the size matching wouldn’t connect. El Duendes’ are accused of kidnapping small children and bringing them to temples hidden deep in forests. Others believe that they protect lost hikers, lost children, and animals of the forests.

Their existence has yet to be proven and there is no speculation of this creature being real, but folks in Latin America believe El Duende is very real and very serious. Whether El Duende is a protector or evil, if you hear one whistle, it’s best you get as far as you can in whatever forest you stumbled upon before you get lost for good.

El Duendes' height is around three to four feet tall, and is believed to be part of the primate species. It has heavy shoulders, long arms and long hair from the neck and back. They are typically seen wearing big hats and look like small humans.
