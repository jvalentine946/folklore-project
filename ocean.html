Kraken

The Kraken is a legendary sea monster often described as a giant, octopus- or squid-like creature with massive tentacles capable of dragging entire ships beneath the waves. Estimates in folklore range from 40 to over 100 feet, or ever bigger.

The first recorded sighting of the Kraken dates back to the 12th century, appearing in Norse sagas and later accounts by Scandinavian sailors. Early accounts of another massive aquatic creature called the Hafgufa predate the Kraken, and may be the basis for the Kraken legend.

The Kraken is best known for its fearsome reputation in maritime folklore as a powerful and mysterious creature that attacks ships, creating whirlpools and destruction in its wake. It is typically said to dwell off the coasts of Norway and Greenland, lurking in the deep ocean.

In some legends, sailors would attempt to anchored to it, thinking due to its size that it was an isalnd, only to realize as it began to drag their ship under that it was alive.
